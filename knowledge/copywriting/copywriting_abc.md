To successfully write copy for a B2B SaaS product in an immature market, you must focus heavily on educating the buyer, breaking their reliance on the status quo, and overcoming deep skepticism. Because the market is immature, prospects may not fully understand their problem yet, and they certainly don't know your solution category exists
.
Here is an end-to-end framework based on the best practices of top conversion copywriters:
Phase 1: Research and Discovery (Voice of Customer)
In an immature market, you cannot rely on established industry terms because they don't exist yet. You must "eavesdrop" on your prospects and steal their exact language
.
The Golden Question: Ask your early adopters, "What was going on in your life that brought you to try/buy [Product] today?"
. This reveals the exact trigger that made them seek a solution.
Identify the JTBD (Jobs-To-Be-Done): B2B buyers don't buy software for the features; they "hire" the software to get a specific job done
. Shift your focus from what your product is to what it helps the user achieve
.
Define the BDF (Beliefs, Desires, Feelings): Map out the "Core Complex" of your target audience. What do they believe about their current workflow? What do they desire to change? How do they feel (e.g., overwhelmed, frustrated by manual tasks)?
.
Review Mining: If you lack customers, mine reviews of the "status quo" solutions or alternative workarounds (like Reddit threads or Amazon reviews of related business books) to find "sticky language" and uncover hidden frustrations
.
Phase 2: The Structural Framework
Because your prospects are in an immature market, you must use a structure that agitates their current pain before pitching your software. Use the Motivating Sequence combined with the PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution) formula
.
1. Get Attention (The Headline) Your headline must capture attention by focusing on a massive, specific benefit
. Rate your headlines using the 4 U's: Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific, and Useful
. In B2B, highly specific numbers (e.g., "Save $5,500 a year") build instant credibility
.
2. Show a Need (The Problem & Agitation) In an immature market, prospects might be aware of a problem but consider it a low priority
. You must dramatize the severity of the problem
.
Mirror their reality: Describe the manual, painful processes they are currently using so accurately that they think, "They understand me perfectly"
.
Agitate the pain: Detail exactly how much time, money, or peace of mind the status quo is costing them
.
3. Position the Solution Introduce your SaaS product as the bridge between their current pain and their desired outcome
.
Translate Features into Outcomes: Do not just list technical specs. Apply the "So What?" test to every feature
. For example, if the feature is "automated syncing," the outcome is "never manually update a spreadsheet again."
4. Prove It (Credibility & Risk Reversal) Since the market is immature, buyers will be highly skeptical
. You must prove your claims
.
Provide specific data, case studies, and testimonials from peers
.
Proactively address objections: Acknowledge their fears (e.g., implementation time, security) head-on within the copy to build trust
.
Offer a strong risk-reversal, such as a free trial or money-back guarantee, to make taking the next step feel safe
.
5. Ask for Action (The CTA) Tell the reader exactly what to do next
. Tie your Call to Action to the outcome they will receive after clicking, rather than just using a generic "Sign Up" button
.
Phase 3: Writing and Tone
Use Conversational B2B Copywriting: B2B marketing is still human-to-human
. Write as if you are having a meeting with the prospect
. Avoid formal, stiff, "corporate" jargon that makes your brand invisible
.
Use the Right Jargon: Only use industry-specific technical language if it proves you are an insider and if 95% of your audience will instantly understand it. Otherwise, explain it in plain English
.
Write the "Zero Draft": Don't stare at a blank page. Take your Voice of Customer research and simply organize the raw data and quotes onto the page according to your PAS structure
.
Phase 4: The Editing Sweeps
Great conversion copywriters edit heavily. Apply Joanna Wiebe's "7 Sweeps" to refine your draft
:
Clarity Sweep: Is the core message completely obvious?
Voice & Tone Sweep: Does the mood match the brand and what the prospect needs to feel?
"So What?" Sweep: Have you translated every feature into a meaningful business outcome?
Prove It Sweep: Is every claim backed up by data, a screenshot, or a testimonial?
Specificity Sweep: Can you replace vague words with exact numbers or concrete details?
Heightened Emotion Sweep: Can you make them feel the pain or the relief more deeply?
Zero Risk Sweep: Have you eliminated all friction and addressed every objection before the CTA?
By rooting your copy entirely in customer research and structuring it to disrupt their comfortable status quo, you will effectively educate an immature market and drive them to adopt your SaaS solution
