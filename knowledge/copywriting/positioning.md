# Positioning guidance

- Position against the status quo, not just against competitors.
- Translate features into an operational change the buyer will care about.
- The message should connect to a buying problem already visible in the account research.
- Specificity beats adjectives. Mention the workflow, team, or motion that changes.
- Good outbound positioning often sounds like:
  - "When team growth creates inconsistent execution, we help standardize the work upstream."
  - "Instead of asking reps to research and write from scratch, the system gives them a grounded starting point."
- Do not overload the email with every feature. Pick one strong wedge tied to the account context.
- Proof should support the positioning, not distract from it.
